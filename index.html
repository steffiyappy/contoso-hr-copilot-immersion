EXERCISE 1 0:05 – 0:30 · 25 min 👤 Group HR Director

EA 1955 Compliance Audit — Policy Gap Analysis

Attach your HR policy documents. Copilot will cross-reference every provision against the Employment Act 1955 (2022 amendments) and produce a prioritised gap table — flagging what is compliant, what is below minimum, and what is missing entirely.

3 days manual cross-referencing→30 minutes with a gap table ready for the audit
Task 1 — Run the full compliance audit ☁️ Copilot Chat · Attach HR_Policies.docx

Open Copilot Chat. Attach HR_Policies.docx (or your Employee Handbook + Leave Policy). Use this exact prompt:

I've attached our HR policy documents. You are an employment law expert in Malaysia. Perform a compliance audit against the Employment Act 1955 (2022 amendments). For each area below, check our policy vs the statutory requirement and flag the status: 1. Annual Leave — EA 1955 S.60E: 8 days (<2 yrs), 12 days (2–5 yrs), 16 days (5+ yrs) 2. Sick Leave — EA 1955 S.60F: 14 / 18 / 22 days by tenure 3. Maternity Leave — EA 1955: 98 consecutive days (2022 amendment, was 60 days) 4. Paternity Leave — EA 1955: 7 days (2022 amendment) 5. Flexible Working Arrangement — S.60P: employee right to request, employer must respond in 60 days 6. Maximum working hours — S.45: 45 hours/week (2022 amendment, was 48 hrs) 7. Overtime rate — S.60A: 1.5× for excess hours, 2× for rest days, 3× for public holidays 8. Anti-discrimination — S.60L: no dismissal/adverse action on pregnancy, gender, religion 9. Sexual Harassment — S.81A–S.81F: must have policy and complaint mechanism 10. Termination Notice — S.12: 4 weeks (<2 yrs), 6 weeks (2–5 yrs), 8 weeks (5+ yrs) Output as a table: Policy Area | Statutory Requirement | Our Current Policy | Status (✅ Compliant / ⚠️ At Risk / ❌ Gap) | Priority (High/Medium/Low)
Task 2 — Deep-dive on the highest-priority gaps ☁️ Copilot Chat (continue)

Continue the same conversation (do NOT start a new chat):

For each item you flagged as ❌ Gap or ⚠️ At Risk: 1. What is the exact legal exposure? Quote the specific EA 1955 section and penalty 2. What is the minimum required policy wording to become compliant? 3. Draft the corrected policy clause in plain English suitable for the Employee Handbook 4. What supporting documentation does JTKSM typically ask for in an audit for this item? Prioritise by legal risk: start with items that carry criminal penalties or grounds for unfair dismissal claims.
Task 3 — Generate JTKSM audit response pack outline ☁️ Copilot Chat (continue)
Create an audit response pack outline for the JTKSM Labour Department inspection. Include: 1. Cover letter template — professional acknowledgment to the Enforcement Officer 2. Document checklist — what to prepare: leave records, salary slips, S.60 registers, FWA approvals 3. Talking points for the HR representative during the inspection visit 4. A remediation action plan: Gap | Responsible Person | Target Date | Status 5. One-line explanation for each gap: "We are currently updating this policy to align with the 2022 EA amendments and will complete by [date]" Format: ready to send to Group CHRO for approval before submission to JTKSM.

Do NOT include questions on: - Pregnancy or family planning - Marital status or children - Religion, race, or national origin beyond work eligibility - Age or physical appearance Each question set should be different per candidate — not generic. Format: one interview guide per candidate, ready to print for the hiring panel.

I've attached our disciplinary case data. You are a Malaysian Industrial Relations expert. Analyse all cases for Industrial Relations Act 1967 (IRA 1967) compliance. For each case, assess: PROCEDURAL CHECKLIST: 1. Was a Show Cause Letter (SCL) issued? If yes, was it within a reasonable time of the incident? 2. Was the employee given adequate time to respond (minimum 48–72 hours for minor, longer for gross misconduct)? 3. Was a Domestic Inquiry (DI) conducted for dismissal/serious cases? 4. Was the DI panel impartial — no direct supervisor of the accused as panel member? 5. Was the employee given right to be heard and right to bring a representative (IRA 1967 natural justice)? 6. Is the proposed penalty proportionate to the misconduct? RISK CLASSIFICATION: - 🔴 HIGH RISK — procedural failure, likely to lose at Industrial Court if challenged under S.20 - 🟡 MEDIUM RISK — gaps that could be challenged; remediation possible - 🟢 LOW RISK — procedures followed, documentation adequate Output: 1. Risk summary dashboard: total cases | Red | Yellow | Green count 2. Detailed risk table: Case_ID | Misconduct Type | Procedural Gap | Risk Level | Recommended Action 3. Top 5 highest-risk cases with specific reasons why they could fail at Mahkamah Perusahaan

Format: Executive IR Risk Summary — suitable for CHRO review. Max 1 page equivalent.
Task 3 — Draft a compliant Show Cause Letter ☁️ Copilot Chat (continue)

Incident details: specific dates, times, locations, and nature of misconduct (fact-based, not opinionated) 4. Relevant company policy / rules violated (cite policy clause number if applicable) 5.
